Inspiration and process of my portfolio:

At the start of designing the background for my portfolio, I began by looking for inspiration. I realized that this portfolio should connect with who I am and reflect my personality, so I thought about combining my interest in history and royal dynasties with a modern, clean design. I’ve always admired the "fleur-de-lis" symbol, a classic emblem used frequently in medieval heraldry. I came across a background on Adobe Stock featuring a fleur-de-lis pattern, which I tried using, but I noticed it didn’t quite fit my vision and had a watermark. So, I decided to design my own background on Canva, to make it feel more personal and fitting with my concept. I believe it’s essential in this industry to stand out and bring a unique touch as a specialist.

Conclusion:

This semester, I’ve learned how to effectively apply the iterative design process to create meaningful, user-friendly designs. By consistently refining my work based on feedback and research, I was able to improve the quality of my projects and gain valuable skills in both design and collaboration. The BLIK project taught me the importance of testing different layouts and color schemes, while my personal portfolio allowed me to implement direct feedback to refine the user experience. Additionally, the Monk Development project emphasized the significance of user-friendly interfaces, such as filters for easier navigation. Through this process, I’ve also gained a deeper understanding of how to incorporate personal style and modern design elements into my work. Going forward, I plan to continue refining my designs and implementing feedback to ensure continuous improvement in my work. The knowledge and skills I’ve developed this semester will serve as a solid foundation for future projects in web design and interactive media.
